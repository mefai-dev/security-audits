## Executive Summary

The risk is **NOT in the code, it is in the economics and the marketing metrics.** The
project advertises **$654M total volume** and **$1.54M TVL** while the entire DEX holds
**under $1M of real liquidity**, and the TOPAZ token is realistically only sellable on
Topaz's own venue with **~$137K of TOPAZ side liquidity**. The volume figures show
**daily turnover ratios of 11x to 80x per pool**, versus ~1x for the busiest legitimate BSC
concentrated liquidity pool, which are strong indicators of **inorganic / wash traded volume**
rather than genuine demand, and are produced by a DefiLlama adapter the **team authored
themselves** with no wash filter.

This produces a classic **"real product, fake traction"** profile: a working DEX whose
headline numbers are inflated to attract deposits, while exit liquidity for ordinary holders
is dangerously thin.

**Two findings are confirmed directly on chain:**
1. **Centralization is absolute.** Every privileged role, `feeManager`, `pauser`,
   `governor`, Minter `team`, `CLFactory owner`, `VotingEscrow team`, resolves to **one
   wallet**, `0xf407…540c`, a **Gnosis Safe with threshold 1 of 3**. A single one of three
   signers can, with **no timelock**, change any pool's fee (0 to 3%), pause swaps on any pool,
   and act as protocol governor. The docs' separately named roles are a single key in practice.
2. **TOPAZ is practically only sellable on Topaz, confirmed.** Direct on chain measurement shows
   that the only external venue, a **Uniswap V4 singleton on BSC**, holds **only 6,986.83 TOPAZ
   (~$2.93) across ALL its pools**, it is dust, unindexed, and cannot absorb any real position.
   For every practical purpose the community claim is correct: there is **no real external
   market**; holders can only exit into the ~$137K of team controlled on Topaz liquidity.

## 1. Inflated / Wash Traded Volume Metrics

**Severity: HIGH**

**CLAIM:**
> "Total Volume $654.36M … Real liquidity. Real yield.", topazdex.com homepage

**REALITY:**
The all time volume is real on paper (DefiLlama confirms `Topaz CL totalAllTime = $650,534,467`)
but it is mathematically implausible as **organic** volume given the liquidity backing it.
The DEX's volume adapter starts **2026-05-16** (~5.4 weeks before this audit) and the venue holds
**under $1M total liquidity**, yet reports **$654.7M all time**, i.e. **~$17M/day averaged over
its entire ~38 day life** (DefiLlama's most recent day prints ~$20M). Reconciliation:
`$654.7M ÷ 38 days ≈ $17.2M/day`.

**The volume figure is self reported.** The `$654.7M` is produced by a DefiLlama dimension adapter
that the **team wrote and submitted themselves**, `github.com/topazdex/dimension-adapters`,
file `dexs/topaz/index.ts`. Reading the adapter source:
- `start: '2026-05-16'`, i.e. the entire "all time" history is ~5 to 6 weeks old.
- It pulls the v2 `Swap` topic `0xb3e2773606abfd…ffaf75b` from the team's own
  `PoolFactory 0x65E6cD0e…` and sums **gross notional**:
  `amount0 = amount0In + amount0Out`, `amount1 = amount1In + amount1Out`.
- There is **no wash trade filter, no unique trader weighting, no self trade exclusion** of any
  kind. Every swap, including a wallet trading with itself back and forth, counts at full size.
- It also reports `dailyFees`, and then sets `dailyRevenue = dailyHoldersRevenue = dailyFees`,
  inflating the "revenue" line from the same unfiltered swap stream.

In fairness, **every** DefiLlama adapter is project submitted, self authoring is normal and not
itself evidence of fraud; DefiLlama's peer review checks that the code reads on chain state, not
that the underlying flow is organic. The problem here is specifically that **this** adapter, by
design, **cannot distinguish a wash trade from a real one** and counts gross notional, so the
$654.7M is best read as *unfiltered raw swap throughput on team controlled pools*, not validated
organic demand. The wash conclusion rests on the turnover ratios and balanced buy/sell counts
below, not on the mere fact that the team wrote the adapter.

**EVIDENCE, live wash signal (DexScreener, TOPAZ token, 2026-06-23):**

| Pool | Liquidity | 24h Volume | Daily Turnover | Txns 24h |
| :--- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| TOPAZ / WBNB `0x71F0953e…` | $133,336 | $5,757 | 0.04x | 161 buy / 180 sell |
| TOPAZ / WBNB `0x1fF00B9c…` | **$1,151** | **$12,817** | **~11x** | 263 buy / 423 sell |

A **$1,151** pool reporting **$12,817** of 24h volume = **~11x its entire depth churned in one
day**, on 686 nearly balanced buy/sell transactions. That is not organic price discovery; it is
a wallet (or wallets) cycling the same tiny float to manufacture "activity." Earlier
GeckoTerminal snapshots of the stablecoin/major pools showed the same pattern venue wide
(WBNB/USDT $74K→$5.88M = 79x; ETH/USDT 68x; BTCB/USDT 49x; USD1/USDT 41x), with violent
day over day swings (DefiLlama `change_1d = +76.1%` CL / `+112.3%` AMM).

**Benchmark against legitimate BSC concentrated liquidity pools (PancakeSwap V3, same day):**

| Pool | Liquidity | 24h Vol | Turnover |
| :--- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| PCS WBNB/USDT (flagship) | $33.5M | ~$2M | **0.06x** |
| PCS CAKE/WBNB (most active) | n/a | n/a | **1.04x** |
| PCS CAKE/USDT | n/a | n/a | **0.13x** |
| **Topaz pool2 `0x1fF00B9c…`** | **$1,151** | **$12,817** | **~11x** |

Even the *most active* legitimate BSC CL pool turns over ~1x/day; Topaz's pool2 turns over **~11x**,
roughly **10x beyond anything organic**, on **~$19 average micro trades**. This is the decisive
rebuttal to the concentrated liquidity caveat below: CL elevation cannot produce a 10x gap over
the busiest real CL pool on the chain.

> Caveat (fairness): concentrated liquidity pools genuinely show higher volume/TVL than v2
> pools because liquidity is concentrated, and `reserve_in_usd` understates effective depth.
> This explains *some* elevation, it does **not** plausibly explain a uniform 40 to 80x across
> the whole venue, an 11x turnover on a $1.1K book that exceeds the busiest legitimate BSC CL
> pool by ~10x, or a self authored adapter with no wash filter.

> Methodology note: per address on chain swap sampling (`eth_getLogs` over the two TOPAZ pools)
> was attempted to attribute the wash flow to specific wallets, but every free BSC RPC endpoint
> rejected the log range (`-32005 limit exceeded` / `403 Forbidden`). The conclusion therefore
> rests on (a) the adapter source code, (b) liquidity vs volume turnover ratios, and (c) the
> balanced buy/sell transaction counts, all independently pointing to wash activity.

**IMPACT:**
Users and aggregators are misled into believing Topaz is a deep, highly used venue. Because the
reporting pipeline is team owned and unfiltered, the $654.7M can be inflated at will. Inflated
volume is used to justify emissions, attract LP deposits, and lure traders who then face thin
real liquidity and slippage.

## 2. Thin Liquidity & Single Venue Lock In for TOPAZ

**Severity: HIGH**

**CLAIM:**
> "TVL $1.54M", and the implicit promise that TOPAZ is a liquid, tradeable asset.

**REALITY (on chain verified, the community report is essentially correct):**
TOPAZ liquidity is **almost entirely concentrated on Topaz's own venue** (DexScreener indexes
**only** `dexId: topaz` pairs across all chains/DEXes, ~$137K total TOPAZ side depth). The only
external route is a **Uniswap V4 singleton on BSC** (Pool Manager
`0x28e2ea090877bf75740558f6bfb36a5ffee9e9df`), and **direct on chain measurement proves that
"external market" is dust**: `TOPAZ.balanceOf(PoolManager)` = **6,986.83 TOPAZ**.

> Methodology (V4 singleton): in Uniswap V4 every pool's reserves live inside the one PoolManager,
> so `balanceOf(PoolManager)` returns TOPAZ across **all** V4 pools at once. That makes it a valid
> **upper bound** on total external V4 depth, no individual V4 pool can hold more TOPAZ than the
> singleton's entire balance. 6,986.83 TOPAZ is therefore the most TOPAZ the *whole* of Uniswap V4
> could possibly absorb, and a realized swap through it confirms the effective figure is far lower.

Valued at the on Topaz quote ($0.003641) that ceiling is ≈ **$25**; valued at the price actually
realizable when selling into near zero depth ($0.00042/token) it is ≈ **$3**. Either way it is
pocket change, and the ~8.6x gap between the two prices is itself proof there is no arbitrage
depth connecting Topaz to any outside venue. That is not a market; it is a stub pool with
essentially zero depth, and it is **not even indexed by DexScreener**.
For all practical purposes the community claim holds: **TOPAZ is only sellable on Topaz's own
DEX.** A holder cannot exit any meaningful position anywhere else, the entire off Topaz venue
could be drained for under three dollars.

**EVIDENCE (DexScreener, TOPAZ token `0xdf00...8Abd`):**

| Pair | DEX | Liquidity | 24h Vol | Buys/Sells |
| :--- | :--- | ---: | ---: | :--- |
| TOPAZ / WBNB | topaz | $132,121 | $5,989 | 163 / 180 |
| TOPAZ / WBNB | topaz | $4,078 | $12,591 | 263 / 420 |
| SPCXon / TOPAZ | topaz | $158 | $627 | 46 / 46 |

**EVIDENCE (on chain depth measurement, `eth_call balanceOf`):**
- `balanceOf(TOPAZ, 0x28e2ea09…)` = **6,986.83 TOPAZ**, the TOTAL TOPAZ held by the Uniswap V4
  Pool Manager across every V4 pool combined. At ~$0.00042 that is **≈ $2.93** of depth.
- A realized swap routed through this V4 singleton (Uniswap V4, 2 hops) priced TOPAZ at an
  effective ≈ **$0.00042/token**, an ~8.6x discount to the on Topaz quote of $0.003641, which
  confirms the near zero external depth measured above.
- The token contract (`Topaz.sol`) is a clean Aerodrome fork, **no transfer tax, no blacklist,
  no transfer pause**, so the lock in is **economic, not coded**: liquidity is only meaningfully
  seeded on Topaz's own venue, which the team controls via gauges/emissions, and no third party
  has bothered to seed a real external pool (the only off Topaz pool is a $3 stub).

**IMPACT:**
This is the core **exit liquidity risk**, and the community report's headline claim is
**confirmed**: holders can only realistically sell into the ~$137K of liquidity the project
itself controls and can re weight via gauge votes/emissions. The single off Topaz venue (Uniswap
V4) is dust (~$3) and cannot absorb any real position. If the team withdraws liquidity or stops
directing emissions, holders are **effectively trapped with no external exit**. This directly
matches the community report ("people trade without knowing and lose money; TOPAZ can only be
sold on Topaz").

## 7. Fee Engine: Asymmetric Advantage for Insiders & Tunable Sell Friction

**Severity: MEDIUM** *(code level, line by line traced)*

**FINDING:** There is **no hidden honeypot sell tax** in the token or pool, and no mechanism that
mechanically forces ordinary users to sell at a loss. What the code does contain is the
**Slipstream dynamic fee engine, which gives the team three real, on chain levers** that
disadvantage ordinary users relative to insiders. Fully traced:

```
CLPool.swap()                       L719: fee = fee()   // read ONCE at swap start
  → CLPool.fee()                    L181-182: return CLFactory.getSwapFee(pool)
    → DynamicSwapFeeModule.getFee() L182-205:
        totalFee = baseFee + (|currentTick − TWAP_tick| × scalingFactor / 1e6)
        totalFee = min(totalFee, feeCap)                 // feeCap ≤ 5% (MAX_FEE_CAP=50_000)
        if discounted[tx.origin] > 0:                    // L199
            totalFee -= totalFee × discount / 1e6        // discount ≤ 50% (MAX_DISCOUNT=500_000)
```

**Lever 1, Dynamic fee penalizes price moving (large) trades.**
`_getDynamicFee` (L207 225) charges extra in proportion to how far the price has moved from the
10 minute TWAP. A large sell that pushes price down pays a higher fee (up to the 5% cap). This is
a legitimate anti volatility design inherited from Velodrome, **but the team (`swapFeeManager`)
sets `scalingFactor` and `feeCap` per pool** (`setScalingFactor`, `setFeeCap`, L97 113), so it can
**tune how punitive sell side friction is, pool by pool.**

**Lever 2, Discriminatory discount whitelist (the real asymmetry).**
`registerDiscounted(addr, discount)` (L163 168) lets the team grant **any address up to a 50% fee
discount**, applied by **`tx.origin`** (L199). Effect: the team's own market maker / wash trading
bots and favored insiders can trade at **half the fee** ordinary users pay. This both (a) makes
manufacturing volume (Finding #1) cheap for insiders and (b) structurally disadvantages retail.
Using `tx.origin` for pricing is also a known anti pattern.

**Lever 3, No timelock; all fee roles collapse to one 1 of 3 Safe (CONFIRMED on chain).**
`CLFactory` L47 49 sets `owner = swapFeeManager = unstakedFeeManager = msg.sender` at deploy;
`setSwapFeeManager` (L108 113) is callable only by the current manager (self perpetuating).
On chain today: **`CLFactory.owner()` and `PoolFactory.feeManager()` both = `0xf407…540c`**,
a **Gnosis Safe with threshold 1 of 3** (owners `0x8c8d…1190`, `0x2347…5225`, `0xd7e8…4aea`).
**Any single one of three signers can change every pool's fee, discount whitelist, and pause
state instantly, no timelock, no on chain delay, no multi party requirement in practice.**

**What it is NOT:** the pool swap math (L682 755) is standard Uniswap v3; fee is fixed per swap
(no mid swap sandwich injected by the contract); the base/dynamic fees are bounded (3% base /
5% cap). So this is **not** a classic unlimited sell tax honeypot.

**IMPACT:**
The team and whitelisted insiders can trade at up to 50% lower fees than the users they onboard,
and can dial up sell side fees per pool. Combined with #1 (wash volume) and #4 (50% veTOPAZ),
this is a system where **insiders operate on materially better terms than retail**, the
code level counterpart to the community complaint that "people trade without knowing and lose
money." The three levers are present and exercisable on chain today: the `swapFeeManager` /
`discounted[...]` whitelist and per pool `feeCap` / `scalingFactor` setters are all controlled by
the single 1 of 3 Safe `0xf407…540c` (confirmed in Finding #8), with no timelock between a role
holder deciding to use them and the change taking effect.
